Tulsi - The Holy Basil

About Tulsi

Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum), also known as Holy Basil, is a sacred medicinal herb widely used in Ayurvedic medicine. This aromatic perennial plant grows 30-60 cm tall with purple to reddish stems and green, oval-shaped leaves with slightly toothed edges. The leaves emit a strong clove-like scent due to their essential oils. Tulsi has remarkable therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and adaptogenic effects. It's traditionally used to treat respiratory conditions, reduce stress, boost immunity, and lower blood sugar levels. In Hindu culture, Tulsi is considered sacred and is often grown in homes and temples. The plant requires well-draining soil, regular sunlight, and moderate watering. Its leaves can be consumed fresh, dried, or as tea to harness its medicinal benefits.
